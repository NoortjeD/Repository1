Cultural Strategy Assignment

Case

You are working at the multinational company Unilever which operates in 190 countries with 400 brands. You will join the Dutch head of marketing in a meeting with 3 managers from different societies (by your own choice). Your goal; get everyone on board to sell a new product.


My Answer

(I will meet with managers from the United States, Latvia, and Argentina.)

I would send them all an invitation for the meeting, being respectful, and professional. Not too personal. I would both mention personal benefits, and benefits for us all, since Individualism is quite different per country. I would clearly write down the plan, and what is going to happen if everyone would agree, since all cultures like to avoid uncertainty.



My Leader Role Assignment

Assignment

Create a culture ad based on 1 dimension and 2 societies by your own choice, based on the work environment.



My Ad Concept

(I will base this ad on the dimension ‘Individualism vs. collectivism’, and the Chinese, and American societies.)

We are located in the park. A group of American men sit at a big picknick table. They are informally dressed, and they all have their own lunch, except for one, who forgot his. A moment later, a group of Chinese people joins them at the table. They notice that one guy doesn’t have food, and they start sharing their food with him. The other Americans look confused and a bit judgemental, but after seeing the Chinese and their fellow American eating together, they share their food as well. The ad ends with all people sharing their food together.



Iceberg Model Hall Assignment

Assignment

Have you even been in a situation in which stereotyping took place? Or have you even seen a situation, like in the video, in which people were stereotypes? Write your experience in the box below.



My Answer

The first thing that came to my mind, is something that regularly happens at work. I work at the supermarket, where you sometimes have to lift heavy loads. Because I am a woman, certain men don’t think I am strong enough to do things by myself. Once, for example, I was filling crates of beer. One of the managers of the store jokingly asked me why I was the one filling those, in stead of a male co-worker, and continued to ‘help’ me out by filling some of the crates himself. I know he meant it as a joke, and it is nice of him to help me. But his ‘help’ was completely based on the stereotype that women aren’t strong and need help from men. That is not true at all because I could have done it by myself without any struggle or problems.

Luckily this was a while ago, and I don’t remember a lot of other interactions like that.
